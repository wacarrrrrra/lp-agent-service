# DataHub Brand Voice — Quick Reference

> Full guide: `docs/DataHub Editorial Style Guide.md`

---

## The DataHub Voice

Sound like an experienced architect explaining why foundations matter.

- Confident, not arrogant
- Substantive, not erudite
- Precise technical language when it matters; plain language everywhere else
- Direct about trade-offs and implementation realities
- Lead with practitioner insights and concrete outcomes — not marketing claims
- Strategic clarity over buzzwords; prove value through specific examples

---

## Writing Rules (Enforce on Every Page)

### Tone
- Start with the audience's problem — not what you want them to do
- Address the reader directly with "you" where natural
- No hype, no overselling, no vague "AI-powered" claims
- All CTAs and headlines must reflect the actual value proposition — no clickbait

### Copy
- Active voice always ("DataHub connects your data" not "your data is connected by DataHub")
- Strong action verbs — marketing is about inspiring action
- Rule of three for rhythm — use sparingly, not in every sentence
- Never use a long word where a short one works
- Cut every word that doesn't earn its place
- No foreign phrases, scientific words, or jargon where a plain equivalent exists

### What to Avoid
- Exclamation marks (unprofessional; exception: quotes and social media only)
- Em dashes used frequently — overused by AI; use intentionally or not at all
- Semicolons — use a period instead
- Passive voice
- Weasel words ("many," "significant," "leading") — quantify or cut
- Minimizing language: "just," "simply," "easy"

## Grammar Shortcuts

| Rule | Do | Don't |
|---|---|---|
| Numbers | Spell out one–nine; numerals for 10+ | "4 use cases" |
| Headlines | Sentence case | Title Case (except H1) |
| Commas | Oxford/serial comma always | "dogs, grandma and grandpa" |
| Organizations | "it/its" not "they/their" | "Netflix raised their targets" |
| Acronyms | Write out on first use: Model Context Protocol (MCP) | Undefined abbreviations |
| Dates | March 3, 2026 | 3/3/26 |
| Ampersand | Write "and" — never & | "Sales & Marketing" |

---

## Accessibility
- All images must have alt text — descriptive, not decorative filler
- Avoid "click here" or "learn more" — use specific anchor text
- Use semantic heading hierarchy: one H1, then H2s, then H3s
- No underline, strikethrough, or colored text for emphasis — use bold sparingly
