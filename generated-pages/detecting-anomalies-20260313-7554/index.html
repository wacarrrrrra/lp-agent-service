The real problem

Why detecting anomalies with static rules fails

Static thresholds were built for stable data. Your data is not stable, and the gaps they leave are where incidents are born.

Thresholds break as data evolves

Seasonal traffic, business growth, and new features shift your baselines constantly. Static rules can't keep up, so they either miss real issues or fire on normal variation.

Alert fatigue erodes trust

When every threshold fires on Monday morning volume spikes, engineers stop responding. The alert that matters gets buried in the noise.

Hundreds of tables, one team

Manually tuning thresholds across hundreds of tables is not a strategy. It's a backlog that grows faster than you can clear it.

You find out when customers do

Without proactive detection, the first signal is a broken dashboard or a support ticket. By then, the incident is already in progress.

How DataHub solves it

A better way to detect and resolve data anomalies

ML-powered assertions replace brittle static rules with models that learn your data's actual behavior and improve over time.

Smart Assertions

ML that learns your data's natural patterns

DataHub's Smart Assertions use Prophet-based forecasting trained on your historical data. The model accounts for weekly and monthly seasonality, long-term trends, and normal fluctuations, so alerts fire on real anomalies, not expected variation.

  • Configurable lookback window: 30 to 90-plus days of training data
  • Adjustable sensitivity for precision vs. recall tradeoffs
  • Exclude maintenance windows and holidays from training
Volume detection

Catch row count anomalies early

Volume assertions monitor row count growth and decline patterns across Snowflake, BigQuery, Redshift, and Databricks. When an upstream source goes missing or a duplicate ingestion doubles your table, DataHub flags it before downstream consumers notice.

  • Detects unexpected spikes, drops, and stalled ingestion runs
  • Monitors absolute totals and change rates between pipeline runs
  • Works across partitioned tables with cost-optimized query strategies
Freshness monitoring

Know when your data stops arriving

Freshness assertions track the last-updated timestamp of your tables and alert your team when data stops arriving on schedule. SLA misses surface before analysts open their first dashboard of the day.

  • Configurable SLA windows per table or dataset group
  • Alerts route to Slack, PagerDuty, or your existing incident stack
  • Lineage context shows which downstream assets are at risk
Column and custom SQL

Column-level checks and custom SQL rules

Column assertions catch null rate spikes, distribution shifts, and referential integrity failures at the field level. Custom SQL assertions let your team encode domain-specific rules that no generic threshold could express.

  • Null rate, uniqueness, and value range checks per column
  • Custom SQL assertions for business-logic validation
  • Results stored in the metadata graph for audit and lineage
