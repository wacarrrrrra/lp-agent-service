The real problem

Why detecting anomalies with static rules fails

Manual thresholds break when data patterns shift. The result is alert fatigue, missed incidents, and engineers spending hours on root cause instead of building.

Rules break when patterns shift

Seasonal spikes, growth trends, and schema changes make static thresholds obsolete. Every change requires a manual update across hundreds of tables.

Data anomalies hide in coverage gaps

New datasets appear without monitoring. No one owns the configuration. Critical tables go unobserved until a stakeholder reports a broken dashboard.

Alert fatigue erodes trust

High false-positive rates train your team to ignore notifications. The one real incident gets buried in noise, and you find out in the standup.

Scale makes manual config impossible

Configuring rules for every column across thousands of tables is not a workflow. It is a full-time job that still leaves blind spots in your pipelines.

How it works

A better approach to data anomaly detection

DataHub Cloud replaces static rules with ML models that learn your data, scale across your warehouse, and surface only the anomalies that matter.

Smart assertions that learn patterns

DataHub trains ML models on your historical data to establish dynamic baselines. Assertions adapt automatically as your data evolves, without manual reconfiguration.

  • Learns seasonal cycles and long-term growth trends
  • Detects volume, freshness, and distribution shifts
  • No threshold tuning required on initial setup

Monitoring rules at scale

Define a monitor once and propagate it automatically across every matching dataset in your warehouse. New tables inherit coverage the moment they appear.

  • Auto-apply rules to new datasets on ingestion
  • Tag-based propagation across schemas and domains
  • Covers thousands of tables without manual overhead

Tuning and feedback loops

Engineers mark false positives directly in the UI. The model incorporates that signal and adjusts sensitivity, reducing noise over time without rebuilding rules from scratch.

  • One-click false positive feedback from alert view
  • Sensitivity adjusts per dataset based on feedback
  • Alert quality improves continuously over time

In-network execution

Assertions run inside your warehouse compute, not on external infrastructure. Your data never leaves your environment, and execution scales with your existing warehouse capacity.

  • Runs natively in Snowflake, BigQuery, and Redshift
  • No data egress, no external compute dependency
  • Scales automatically with warehouse query capacity

Questions

Frequently asked questions about detecting anomalies

After connecting your warehouse, DataHub begins ingesting metadata and building baseline models from your historical data. The time to first meaningful alerts depends on how much historical data is available and how many tables you are monitoring. For most warehouse configurations, you will see initial assertions running within the first day. Model quality improves over the first few weeks as more historical patterns are incorporated.
The ML models account for seasonality, day-of-week patterns, and growth trends, which eliminates a large category of false positives that static thresholds generate. When a false positive does occur, engineers can mark it directly from the alert view. That feedback is incorporated into the model, and sensitivity adjusts for that specific dataset going forward. The system does not require you to rebuild rules from scratch when your data patterns change.
DataHub Cloud supports in-network assertion execution on Snowflake, BigQuery, Amazon Redshift, and Databricks. Assertions run inside your warehouse compute, so your data does not leave your environment. For other sources in the DataHub catalog, metadata-based monitoring is available through the standard ingestion framework. The list of supported platforms for in-network execution is updated as new integrations are released.
Assertions execute inside your warehouse using your existing compute. DataHub does not pull raw row-level data out of your environment. What DataHub stores is assertion results, aggregate statistics, and metadata about your datasets. For organizations with strict data residency requirements, the self-hosted deployment option keeps all metadata storage within your own infrastructure as well.
The auto-propagation feature is designed specifically for this. You define a monitor template once, and DataHub applies it automatically to every dataset that matches your tag, domain, or schema criteria. New tables that appear after the rule is created inherit coverage immediately. Execution scales with your warehouse capacity, so there is no separate compute layer to provision or manage as your table count grows.
