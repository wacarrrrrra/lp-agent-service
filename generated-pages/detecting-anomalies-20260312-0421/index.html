The problem

Why detecting data anomalies keeps breaking your on-call rotation

Static threshold rules made sense when you had 20 tables. They don't scale to 2,000. And they certainly don't account for the fact that your Monday row counts look nothing like your Saturday row counts.

Alert fatigue is real

When every threshold violation pages your team, engineers start ignoring alerts. The one that matters gets buried.

Seasonal patterns break rules

A 40% drop in weekend traffic is normal. A 40% drop on a Tuesday is a crisis. Static rules can't tell the difference.

Rules at scale drain teams

Writing, maintaining, and updating assertions for hundreds of tables consumes engineering hours that should go toward building.

You find out last

Downstream consumers often discover data quality issues before the platform team does. That's a trust problem, not just a technical one.

The cost of inaction isn't just broken dashboards. It's the accumulated credibility loss every time a data incident surfaces in a meeting you weren't in. There's a better way to approach data anomaly detection—one that doesn't require you to predict every failure mode in advance.

The solution

A better way to automate anomaly detection in data

DataHub's Smart Assertions use machine learning to learn the historical patterns of your data—including day-of-week seasonality and long-term trends—and flag deviations automatically. No manual threshold configuration. No rules that go stale.

Smart assertions

Assertions that learn your data's normal

Instead of asking you to define what "wrong" looks like, DataHub learns what "right" looks like from your historical data. The ML inference engine accounts for seasonality, growth trends, and cyclical patterns—so a weekend dip doesn't page your team, but a Tuesday anomaly does.

When the model gets it wrong, you tell it. Mark a false positive as expected, and the model updates. Mark a missed anomaly as a real issue, and sensitivity increases. The system improves with every data refresh.
