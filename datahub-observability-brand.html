FAQ

Frequently asked questions about data observability

Most teams connect their first data source and have monitors running within a few hours. DataHub's 100+ pre-built integrations cover the most common warehouse and pipeline tools. The platform auto-discovers your assets and suggests monitors based on observed data patterns — no manual configuration needed to get meaningful coverage quickly.
Smart Assertions use ML to learn your data patterns and adapt thresholds automatically. You can subscribe to specific datasets and assertion types relevant to your ownership area. The Data Health Dashboard provides filtered views by priority and team — so engineers focus on what actually requires their attention, not a firehose of noise.
DataHub integrates natively with 100+ tools including Snowflake, Databricks, BigQuery, Redshift, dbt, Airflow, Spark, Kafka, Looker, and Tableau. Native connectors capture lineage, execution status, and metadata automatically — without additional instrumentation on your side.
DataHub unifies discovery, observability, and governance in a single platform. Quality signals appear in the discovery layer, so analysts can assess reliability when selecting datasets — not after they've built on top of them. Lineage connects incidents to governance policies. You get one source of truth instead of three separate tools that don't share context.
For most environments, implementation means configuring your first connector, reviewing the auto-discovered asset inventory, and enabling monitors. The complexity depends on your stack and how broadly you want to roll out — most teams start narrow (one warehouse, one pipeline tool) and expand from there.
